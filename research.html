Job Market Paper

Pricing Agricultural Environmental Services Under Policy-Consistent Normalizations

Conservation programs differ in how they reward farmers: practice-based programs compensate for adopting management practices, while outcome-based programs pay for measured environmental improvements. We develop a duality-based framework to value agricultural environmental services under policy-consistent normalizations. Using Illinois corn production data, we show that practice-based normalizations result in lower shadow prices for carbon sequestration and greenhouse gas abatement, while outcome-based normalizations produce higher costs. The framework provides a policy-consistent basis for comparing conservation programs and setting incentive payments that align private behavior with environmental goals.

Project 1

What are the carbon services from cover-crop adoption worth from farmers' perspective?

We derive shadow prices of carbon services provided by cover crops relative to non-cover-crop practices, using a data envelopment analysis (DEA) representation of production, carbon sequestration, and greenhouse gas emissions. Cover-crop fields generate carbon sequestration and GHG emission reduction services valued at USD 17.88 and USD 9.42 per acre, respectively. Comparing these values to existing incentive programs highlights the economic feasibility and the barriers to adopting sustainable practices.

Project 2

Agricultural Productivity Index Decomposition with Weather and Adaptation Indicators

Extreme weather events—including heat, drought, and intense rainfall—pose growing risks to agricultural production. This project evaluates how adaptation measures such as cover cropping and conservation tillage help farmers maintain productivity under increasing climatic variability and contribute to long-run resilience in agricultural systems.
