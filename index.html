Nice to Meet You

I'm Saurav Raj Kunwar, an applied economist working on agriculture, climate change, and environmental sustainability. My research uses economic tools to study farm productivity, policy design, and the value of environmental services from agricultural production.

I completed my PhD in Agricultural and Applied Economics at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. I also hold a Master of Science in Agricultural and Applied Economics from the University of Georgia and a Bachelor of Science in Agriculture from Tribhuvan University.

I study the trade-offs between productivity and sustainability in agriculture, with a focus on how conservation practices, weather risk, and policy incentives affect farm performance. My work quantifies the benefits of sustainable practices on soil health, carbon storage, and biodiversity while considering their implications for productivity and profitability.

Using production economics, I estimate shadow prices for environmental services and evaluate incentives that support productive, resilient, and sustainable agricultural systems.
