Saurav Raj Kunwar

I am Saurav Raj Kunwar, an applied economist and Postdoctoral Researcher at Texas Tech University.

My work uses economic theory, applied econometrics, structural modeling, and mathematical programming to study policy design, productivity, risk, and environmental valuation. I am especially interested in how organizations and decision makers can evaluate trade-offs between economic performance, resource use, and long-run sustainability.

I completed my PhD in Agricultural and Applied Economics at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. I also hold an MS in Agricultural and Applied Economics from the University of Georgia and a BS in Agriculture from Tribhuvan University.

What I Work On

Applied Economic Analysis

I translate complex production, resource, and environmental questions into empirical models that support clearer decisions.

Policy and Incentive Design

I evaluate how policies and incentives shape productivity, conservation, adaptation, and environmental outcomes.

Data and Modeling

I work with applied microeconometrics, structural modeling, simulation, big data, and reproducible workflows in R, Stata, Python, GAMS, and related tools.
